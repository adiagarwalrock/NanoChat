## 1\. Introduction

NanoChat is an Android application that enables users to interact with AI models using on-device inference, locally downloaded models, and user-configured remote AI endpoints. This Privacy Policy explains how NanoChat accesses, stores, uses, and discloses information when you use the app.

This Privacy Policy is intended to comply with Google Play requirements and to provide clear notice about the app’s data practices. It should be read together with any disclosures presented in the app and in the Google Play listing.

## 3\. Scope of this Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy applies to the NanoChat Android application distributed through Google Play and to related communications concerning the app.

It does not govern the privacy practices of third party AI providers, device manufacturers, operating system vendors, or websites and services that you choose to connect to NanoChat.

## 4\. Summary of NanoChat’s data practices

NanoChat is designed to prioritize local processing where possible.

Depending on the features you use, NanoChat may:

- store chat history and session metadata locally on your device;
- store app settings and preferences locally on your device;
- store sensitive credentials you enter, such as API keys or access tokens, in encrypted local storage on your device;
- download and store AI model files on your device when you use local model features;
- transmit prompts and related conversation context to a remote AI service only when you explicitly configure and use a remote provider.

NanoChat does **not**:

- require account creation for general use of the app;
- provide any developer-operated proprietary AI endpoint in the current release;
- include advertising SDKs;
- include analytics SDKs;
- include crash reporting SDKs;
- sell personal information;
- share personal information with advertisers or data brokers.

## 5\. Information we process

Depending on how you use NanoChat, the app may process the following categories of information.

### 5.1 User content

This may include:

- prompts, text, and instructions you enter into the app;
- conversation history and chat session titles;
- AI-generated responses;
- model-related inputs you provide, including optional endpoint settings and configuration details.

### 5.2 Configuration and settings data

This may include:

- selected inference mode;
- remote base URL;
- remote model name;
- local model preferences;
- download and storage preferences;
- application settings and UI preferences.

### 5.3 Authentication and secret data

If you choose to use remote AI services or gated model sources, you may enter credentials such as:

- API keys;
- access tokens;
- Hugging Face tokens or similar access credentials.

These credentials are stored locally on your device using encrypted storage mechanisms implemented by the app.

### 5.4 Device and technical data

NanoChat may process limited technical information needed for functionality and security, such as:

- app version;
- Android OS version;
- device capability checks relevant to local inference support;
- local error states generated during app operation.

The current release does not include third party analytics, telemetry, or crash reporting SDKs.

### 5.5 Local model and file data

If you use local model features, NanoChat may download, import, move, delete, or otherwise manage model files on your device storage, including internal or external storage locations supported by Android and selected by you.

## 6\. How we use information

NanoChat uses information only for legitimate app functionality, including to:

- provide chat functionality and display conversations;
- run local on-device inference where supported;
- store and retrieve chat history and session metadata;
- save your app settings and preferences;
- securely store secrets and tokens you choose to enter;
- connect to a remote AI provider you explicitly configure;
- download, import, manage, move, and delete local model files;
- maintain app reliability, security, integrity, and abuse prevention;
- comply with legal obligations and enforce our rights where necessary.

We do not use your information for advertising personalization.

## 7\. Local processing and storage

NanoChat is built to support significant local processing.

In the current app design, chat history and session records are stored locally on the device, and sensitive credentials are stored using encrypted local storage. Locally downloaded model files are stored on the device in locations selected or permitted by the user and Android.

When you use on-device inference modes, your prompts and responses may remain entirely on your device, subject to the behavior and technical constraints of the selected on-device runtime or device component.

## 8\. Remote AI providers and third party processing

NanoChat allows users to configure their own remote AI endpoints. NanoChat does not provide a proprietary developer-hosted inference endpoint in the current release.

If you choose to use a remote AI provider, NanoChat may send the following data to that provider as needed to fulfill your request:

- your prompt text;
- recent conversation history or context included in the request;
- model parameters and configuration values necessary for request execution;
- authentication credentials required by that provider.

When you use a remote provider:

- the provider acts as an independent third party service;
- that provider’s own privacy policy, retention practices, security controls, and terms will apply to data sent to it;
- we do not control how that third party stores, uses, discloses, or retains data once transmitted;
- you are responsible for reviewing and accepting the third party provider’s policies before use.

## 9\. Legal bases and lawful use

To the extent applicable law requires a legal basis for processing, we process information on one or more of the following grounds:

- to perform the app functionality you request;
- with your consent, including where you choose to configure a remote provider or enter credentials;
- for our legitimate interests in operating, securing, and improving the app;
- to comply with legal obligations;
- to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.

## 10\. Data sharing and disclosure

We do not sell personal data. We do not share personal data with advertisers or data brokers.

We may disclose information only in the following circumstances:

1. **At your direction**: when you configure and use a remote AI provider or other third party service.
2. **Service functionality**: when data must be written to local storage locations, device services, or user selected file destinations to enable app features.
3. **Legal compliance**: when required by applicable law, regulation, legal process, court order, or governmental request.
4. **Protection of rights and safety**: when reasonably necessary to protect the rights, property, security, users, developers, or the public.
5. **Business transition**: if the app or related assets are involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, restructuring, dissolution, or asset transfer, subject to applicable law.

## 11\. Data retention

NanoChat is primarily device-local in its retention model.

Unless and until future versions introduce a developer-operated backend, we generally retain data as follows:

- **Chat history and session data:** retained on your device until you delete it in the app, clear app data, remove app storage, or uninstall the app.
- **Settings and preferences:** retained on your device until modified, deleted, cleared through system settings, or removed by uninstalling the app.
- **API keys and tokens:** retained in encrypted local storage until removed, replaced, app data is cleared, or the app is uninstalled.
- **Downloaded or imported model files:** retained on your device until deleted by you through the app, file management tools, clearing storage, or uninstalling the app, subject to Android storage behavior.

Remote providers you configure may retain submitted data according to their own policies. We do not control those retention periods.

## 12\. User controls and deletion

You may manage or delete your data in one or more of the following ways:

- deleting chat history inside the app;
- editing or removing remote configuration and credentials inside the app;
- deleting downloaded or imported model files through the app where supported;
- clearing app data through Android system settings;
- uninstalling the app.

If you contact us regarding a privacy request, we will review the request in accordance with applicable law. However, because the app primarily stores data locally on your device and does not maintain a general developer-hosted account system for user content in the current release, many deletion actions are controlled directly by the user on-device.

## 13\. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards appropriate to the nature of the data and the app’s design. These measures include, where applicable:

- local encrypted storage for sensitive credentials entered by the user;
- limiting data handling to what is necessary for app functionality;
- avoiding third party analytics, ad tech, and crash reporting SDKs in the current release;
- supporting local inference modes to reduce unnecessary network transmission;
- relying on user configured secure endpoints where remote usage is enabled.

No method of storage, device security, or transmission over the internet is completely secure. By using NanoChat, you acknowledge that absolute security cannot be guaranteed.

## 14\. International transfers

If you use a remote AI provider or third party model source, your data may be processed in countries other than your own. Those transfers are governed by the third party provider you select and not by us alone.

By configuring and using such services, you understand that information may be transmitted to and processed in jurisdictions with data protection laws that may differ from those in your country.
